Parkinson's disease (PD) is a neurodegenerative disorder that affects the midbrain, causing a progressive loss of dopamine (DA) neurons. This loss of DA neurons, or dopaminergic deafferentation, can lead to characteristic motor disturbances. Studies have found that brains from Parkinson's patients have over 50% fewer dopaminergic neurons in the midbrain than age-matched controls. [1]
