There are many types of pain.


Acute pain is a sudden, short, sharp stinging sensation. Classified as nociceptive as it is the result of tissue damage. There are two types of noiceptive pain :- somatic and visceral.

Somatic refering to wall of body
pain is localised and sharp. It is the pain you feel when:
  • Bee stings
  • Cut paper grazes
  • Laceration
  • Burns
  • Teeth is plucked
  • Ankle is twisted
Visceral refering to internal organs
pain is a generalised dull pressure/ache. It is the pain you feel due to:
  • Kidney stone
  • Menstrual cramps
  • Appendicitis

Chronic pain is a persistent, pounding, paralysing painful phenomenon. Classified as neuropathic as it is the resutl of nerve damage. It is the pain you feel due to:

  • Poor posture
  • Arthritis
  • Nerve damage
  • Headache (trigeminal neuralgia)
  • Stress (psychogenic pain)
  • Peripheral neuropathy (diabetes, HIV)

inflammatory pain can be chronic or acute depending on the cause.
