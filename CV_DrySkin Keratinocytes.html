Origin of Dry Cells

We all have suffered from those white, flaky patches that never seem to go away.
They swing by every now and then, always ahead of a big day.
Ever wondered why these notorious patches appear?
The answer lies in our skin's stratum corneum(epidermis's uppermost layer).

Let us begin by understanding the layers of our skin; our body's first line of defence.
  • Basal lamina: It separates skin tissue from other tissues and is made of collagen and Laminins.

  • Stratum basale: Consists of stem keratinocyte cells that are cosntantly dividing and are column shaped. Produces keratin and melanin as well.

  • Stratum spinosum: 5 to 10 layers thick and polyhedrally shaped. Extends spines that connect to desmosomes, connecting all the cells.

  • Stratum granulosum: 5 layers thick with diamond shaped cells. Contains lamellar granules.

  • Stratum corneum: Known as horny or corneal layer. The keratinocytes have reached maturity and are preparing for cell death. These dead cells are hard and hence comapred to the hardness of an animal's horn.

Odland bodies, NMF and naturally-occurring lipids are the perfect ingredients to smooth and supple skin.


The keratinocytes(keratin) secrete NMF or Natural Moisturising Factor in the granum layer. These chemicals maintain skin elasitcity, activate hydrolytic enzymes and act as a barrier for our skin. Hydrolytic enzymes then help destroy dead cells and desquamate them or shred them. The NMF is made up of water and humectants, substances that absorb atmospheric water. Thereby, in less humid conditions, lack of water makes the skin dry.

Odland Bodies

Odland bodies are lamellar strucutres that are ovoid shaped and 200-300nm long. They are found in the spinous and granulosum layer. They separate from the Golgi body, move in the cytosol then latch onto the plasma membrane, releasing their contents into the intercellular space. They help maintain anti-microbial immunity, skin desquamation and increase epidermal permeability.

  • Anti-microbial immunity: 2-defensins is a 64 amino acid long protein. Its strong electrostatic charge protects the skin against Gram-negative bacteria. They latch on and increase the pathogen's membrane permeability, killing it. The protein also contains enoguh cysteine which is essential for immuntiy. It acts in places showing inflammatory response. It also triggers stem cells to differnetiate into epithelial cells to regenerate skin.

  • Epidermal permeability: They release polar lipids like glucosylceramides, phospholipids, sphingomyelinase and cholesterol. Enzymes like lipid hydrolases convert these lipid precursors into non polar lipids. Non polar lipids prevent water loss from inside cells. They happen to be surfactants, which means they reduce surface tension of any liquid, helping increase the skin's permeability and absorbancy of skincare. Glycerol is also released from the phospholipid that helps store water.

  • Desquamation: Glycosidases are markers of a cell ready for apoptosis. Cathepsins help in damaging cell organelles. Serine protease decomposes cell adhesion molecules and cleaves peptide bonds. This breaks the intercellular adhesion and hence helps in desquamation

Naturally-occuring lipids

Lipids like cholestrol, ceramides and free fatty acids help maintain water absorbancy, skin elasitcity and prevents flaky skin.

Dry skin is when our body lacks the ability to make these proteins and lipids due to genetic defects or makes less of them. This is where skin care comes in, which contain these as supplements to replenish our skin barrier.
